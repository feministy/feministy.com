Weaving math for rigid heddle looms

I used to have an Ashford rigid heddle loom. At the time, I would weave anywhere from 2-6 pieces a week. I got tired of calculating how much yarn I would need for a project every single time, so I made these tables for reference.

Before you view the data, there are a few things you need to understand first about the weft estimates made:

  • The assumed warp length is always 9 feet (3 yards).
  • The calculations are not exact, they are meant to serve as guidelines. Often times, these calculations are generous. I feel it’s better to be safe than sorry.
  • I have not accounted for shrinkage, fiber content, loom waste, etc. There were too many variables to attach, so I didn’t use them. I would recommend the following additional calculations for less stretchy fabrics: 18 inches for loom waste, 10% shrinkage. For wool/nylon sock blends, you may want to account for additional shrinkage.
  • These calculations are for a rigid heddle loom. I can’t guarantee that they will work with a table or floor loom.
  • The reed sizes are the Ashford sizes.
  • The method of calculation I’m using is one I was taught from a friend. I do not know if it is the best way to calculate weft lengths, but it works for me, despite being a little generous. If you have another method that works for you, I’d recommend sticking to it. If you’re looking for a new method, give my chart a try.
  • The yardage amounts used are based on my personal yarn preferences.

Weft yardage requirements

For all tables: Width is always in inches. Length is always in feet and referrs to the maximum length with the amount of yardage available for the weft. Any reference to yarn (Warp, Weft) is in yards.

Please note that you need to account for shrinkage, loom waste, tension, and other factors, including fiber contents. These calculations are guidelines, not exact formulas. Often times, the weft amount calculated below is generous. Use at your own discretion!
